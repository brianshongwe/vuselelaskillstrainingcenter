Electrical Engineering

Electrical Engineering

Electrical engineering is a dynamic field focusing on the study, design, and application of equipment, devices, and systems that utilize electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It bridges heavy-current electrical energy (power generation) and light-current electronics (information technology, communications, robotics) to create, maintain, and innovate modern technologies.

Plumbing

Plumbing

Plumbing is any system that conveys fluids for a wide range of applications. Plumbing uses pipes, valves, plumbing fixtures, tanks, and other apparatuses to convey fluids.

Welding

Welding

Welding is a fabrication process that joins materials, typically metals or thermoplastics, by using high heat to melt the parts together, often with a filler material, allowing them to cool into a single, solid piece. It is essential for construction, manufacturing, and repair, often using techniques like MIG, TIG, or Stick welding.

Carpentry

Carpentry

Carpentry is a skilled trade focused on cutting, shaping, and installing building materials—primarily wood—for structural frameworks,, staircases, doors, and finishings in construction. It involves high-precision work, utilizing hand and power tools to create both rough, structural elements and finished products. Professional training is available, along with specialized services

Building and construction

Building and construction

Building construction is the process of adding structures to real property, transforming designs into physical reality through planning, site preparation, foundation work, framing, and finishing. It involves coordinating labor, materials, and equipment to create functional, safe, and sustainable buildings such as homes, offices, and industrial facilities.

Mechanical Trades

Mechanical Trades

Mechanical trades are highly skilled professions focused on the installation, repair, and maintenance of complex machinery, infrastructure, and environmental systems, such as HVAC, plumbing, and pipefitting. These trades are essential for building infrastructure, utilizing technical skills to manage systems that provide comfort and safety.
